UK Senior Capstone Project/ESRI Map Book, 2022

For my undergraduate Geography senior capstone project at the University of Kentucky, I wanted to pull from my personal experiences growing up in South Louisville near heavy industry. I would often walk to the bus stop in the morning and smell the putrid odor from the next door industrial facilicties. Using data from the EPA, CDC, and Census Bureau, I explored the connection between historical demographic housing patterns, like redlining in Louisville's west end neighborhoods, and the proxmitity to polluting facilities. By using a bivariate classification scheme, I was able to show multiple critical data sets and types simultanelously, showcasing the concerning trend in the city. In 2022, I attended the ESRI User Conference in San Diego through my job at the PVA. I submitted this project and was selected to be used in ESRI's 2023 Map Book (Volume 3B). As a cartographer, I wish to communicate important issues, such as these, in a easily understood, disgestiable, and attractive way.

PVA Field Map for Assessors, 2023

>When I started working for the PVA in 2021, the orginal field map they used was only 3 layers, being the parcel layer. roads, and buidling outlines. With their limited resources out of the office, I wanted to make a field map that more accuracelty reflected what was actually there in person and provide much needed context to assessors. I used map layers from the Lexington-Fayette Urban County Government (LFUCG), University of Kentucky, KYGEONET, and Openstreetmap, as well as creating/editing my own layera when possible. Orignally, the parcel layer would only get updated a few times a year and had to be created from scratch everytime. Now, the tablet maps are updated twice a month or as needed, and the needed basemap map document is now accessible on a shared space on the network. This new process for the field maps elimnated the frustration assocaited with the parcel layer being outdated and the confusion of a new location
